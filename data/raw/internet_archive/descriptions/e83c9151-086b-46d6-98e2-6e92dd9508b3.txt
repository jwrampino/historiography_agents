The Daily Worker was a newspaper published in the United States by the Daily Worker Pub. Co. from 1924 to 1958. The newspaper served as the United State's Communist Party's main voice, covering labor issues, civil rights, and international affairs from a Marxist perspective. The paper supported Soviet policies and was known for defending workers' rights and exposing capitalist injustices. During the McCarthy era, it faced government scrutiny and declining readership.
