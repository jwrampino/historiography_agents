Boston Commonwealth was a historical publication covering news and current affairs. It was established in 1862 and was published weekly by James M. Stone. The publication served as a voice for loyalist perspectives, often advocating unity and emphasizing the importance of national integrity amidst the tumult of war. Articles frequently included government messages, military updates, and opinion pieces on reconstruction, emancipation, and national policy, making it a valuable resource for understanding the Union's internal discourse during this critical period.
