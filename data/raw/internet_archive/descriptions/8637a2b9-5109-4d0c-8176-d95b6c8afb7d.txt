National PTA bulletin was a newsletter covering topics related to to parent-teacher associations and children’s welfare in the United States. It was established in 1933 and was published 10 times a year by the National Congress of Parents and Teachers until 1974. The National PTA Bulletin was an authoritative periodical dedicated to issues concerning educational advocacy, child welfare, and parent-teacher partnerships. Its scope encompassed legislative concerns, community programs, and resources aimed at fostering effective parental and educational involvement. The publication included monthly and bi-monthly issues featuring articles, reports, and updates on policies affecting children’s health, safety, and education. It also addressed topics such as juvenile delinquency, child labor laws, library services, and public health initiatives, making it a vital resource for PTA leaders, educators, and policymakers.
