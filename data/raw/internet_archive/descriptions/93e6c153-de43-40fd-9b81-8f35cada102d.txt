In the early days of alpha Cat boogie, indeed when we barely knew how to identify ourselves as a group of artists, there existed a sort of drifting mutual philosophy. With Manifest Destiny, Ted Kefton summated such esoterics into a series of soulful, crunchy, beat-laden extropic tracks. The likes of which you will find bear a kindred spirit with wacky space/cave men, distant emotions, and ominous unseen forces. With Buzz as the centerpiece for composition, tracker fans should definitely take note. Gnostic thinkers will enjoy it too.
