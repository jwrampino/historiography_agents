Christian Heritage was a American monthly magazine dedicated to Protestant converts. The magazine was initially published in 1883 under the title; The Converted Catholic, in 1915 it briefly changed its name to the Protestant Review but reverted back to The Converted Catholic in 1919. In 1958 it became Christian Heritage, which it remained until it ceased publication in 1978. The aim of the magazine was to be a resource for men that had chosen to leave the priesthood in favour of Protestantism. It provided articles that damned what they called the militant Roman Catholic clerical fascism of the period.&nbsp; Christian Heritage’s (The Converted Catholic) most notable editor was Leo Herbert Lehman. Born in Ireland in 1895, Lehman entered into the priesthood in 1921. On moving to America Lehman converted to Protestantism and became one of the Catholic Church’s most damning critics, he wrote many articles for the magazine as well as books and pamphlets condemning the the programs and activities of the Roman catholic Church. He believed that ““The strength of the Roman Catholic church lies in the power it has exercised for centuries over the illiterate semi-Christian masses of Eastern Europe and the Latin countries. It has grown fat on their credulity.” In 1958 The Converted Catholic became known as Christian Heritage. The Internet Archive Collection contains microfilm published between 1883 and 1978.
