The most popular free daily in South Korea. Metro Seoul launched in 2002 to coincide with the opening of 2002 Korea/Japan World Cup and Metro Busan came afterwards (aunched in 2003). As the most popular free daily in South Korea, Metro Seoul is distributed every day from underground stations to daily commuters. The average circulation of Metro South Korea is 180,000 with a readership of 226,800. Every morning in over 150 major cities across the world, millions of Metropolitans take to the streets, buses and trains on their daily commute. They are young, well educated trend-setters with healthy media appetites. With unbiased views, engaging features and simple design, Metro has become the preferred source of morning news for this much sought after audience since its launch in early 1995.
