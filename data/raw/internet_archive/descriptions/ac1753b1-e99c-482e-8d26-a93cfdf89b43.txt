The Anti-Slavery Reporter and Aborigines’ Friend was a periodical that covered international anti-slavery movements and notices, anti-slavery movements, indigenous rights, and related social issues. It was established in 1825 and was published monthly by Anti-slavery & Aborigines Protection Society until 2005. It began as a biweekly publication. Each issue had a variety of reports on slave trade activities, insurrections, and legal measures, as well as articles addressing the moral, social, and political implications of slavery and colonial practices. It provided both detailed accounts of atrocities and acts of rebellion, as well as discussions on the ethical underpinnings of abolitionist efforts.
