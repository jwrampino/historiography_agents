New National Era was an African American weekly newspaper published in Washington, D.C., during the Reconstruction Era following the Civil War and the Emancipation Proclamation. It was established in 1870 as a joint venture between two leading anti-slavery and civil rights advocates, J. Sella Martin and Frederick Douglass. Douglass initially served as the editor from 1870 to 1872 before stepping down, at which point his son, Lewis H. Douglass, took over the editorial duties until 1874. The paper focused on advocating for full civil and political rights for African Americans in the aftermath of the Civil War. It featured extensive commentary on current events, political reports, and the activities of the Republican party, which Douglass believed was the best vehicle for advancing Black civil rights at the time.
