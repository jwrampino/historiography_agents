JAMA: The Journal of the American Medical Association is an active publication covering the science and art of medicine. It was established in 1883 and is published weekly 48 times a year by the American Medical Association. The journal aims to promote the science and art of medicine while striving for the betterment of public health across all areas of medicine. It covers a wide range of subjects, including but not limited to clinical research, therapeutic interventions, health policy, medical education, and public health practices. It publishes original research articles, review articles, clinical trials, viewpoints, editorials, and letters to the editor.
