Tamil Virtual University (TVU) was established by the Government of Tamil Nadu on 18th May 2000. This academy is functioning under the administrative control of the Information Technology Department, Government of Tamil Nadu. The TVU was renamed as Tamil Virtual Academy (TVA) with effect from 16th July 2010. The academy aims to provide Internet based Tamil resource access to the Tamil Diaspora across the globe and others who are interested in the Tamil language, literature, art, culture, etc. The Tamil Virtual Academy has created a digital library (www.tamildigitallibrary.in) consisting of rare books, periodicals and palm-leaf manuscripts of various subjects like, literature, religion, history, medicine, science etc., from the 18th century onwards. The digital collections are built from various government and non-governmental organizations such as, Connemara Public Library, Tamil Nadu Archive, Saraswathi Mahal Library, Government Oriental Manuscripts Library, Dr. U.Ve. Saminathiyar Library, Government Museum Library, M.G.R. Film and Television Institute, International Institute of Tamil Studies and Directorate of Indian Medicine & Homeopathy, etc. This library growing as a large digital library in South Asian languages. The library is being enriched continuously by uploading more rare content.
