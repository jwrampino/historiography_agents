This collection contains items from the Mount Sinai Archives, a division of Academic Informatics and Technology at the Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai. The Archives preserves Mount Sinai's institutional records as well as the records of distinguished Mount Sinai staff and alumni. Additional material from the Archives is available in the Mount Sinai Digital Repository . Founded in 1852 as The Jews' Hospital in New York, The Mount Sinai Hospital is one of New York City's oldest hospitals. In 1963, the Hospital received a charter to create a medical school, known today as the Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai. In 2013, the Mount Sinai Health System was created. This comprises The Mount Sinai Hospital, the Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai, and a number of other New York City hospitals including St. Luke's-Roosevelt, the Beth Israel Medical Center, and New York Eye and Ear Infirmary.
