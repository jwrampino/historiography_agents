On April 5, 2025, over 5 million people across the United States and internationally participated in "Hands Off!" protests to oppose policies implemented by President Donald Trump and his adviser, Elon Musk. These policies included aggressive and indiscriminate cost-cutting measures such as mass layoffs of federal employees, significant reductions in social programs like Medicaid and Social Security, and the imposition of new tariffs that contributed to economic instability. Demonstrators also voiced concerns over perceived attacks on civil liberties, LGBTQ+ and immigrant rights, and the increasing influence of billionaires in government affairs. The protests were organized by a coalition of progressive groups, including Indivisible and MoveOn.org, and represented one of the largest mobilizations against the administration during Trump's second term. ​In Washington, D.C., protesters gathered on the National Mall, chanting slogans such as "Hey hey, ho ho, Musk and Trump have got to go!" Many carried signs reflecting their status as federal workers affected by the layoffs. Similar demonstrations took place in cities like Los Angeles, where participants lined busy intersections with signs reading "We the people are pissed" and "Canada not for sale." Internationally, hundreds gathered in European cities including Frankfurt, Berlin, Paris, and London to express solidarity and opposition to the Trump administration's policies. The "Hands Off!" movement aimed to build a massive, visible, national rejection of what organizers described as a hostile takeover of the government by Trump, Musk, and their billionaire allies. They demanded an end to the dismantling of social safety nets, the slashing of public services, and the attacks on various communities. The protests highlighted growing tensions over civil liberties, economic concerns, and foreign policy under the current administration.
