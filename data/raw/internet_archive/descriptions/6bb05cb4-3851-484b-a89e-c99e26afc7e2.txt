New Generation was a publication that covered the issue of child labor. It was established in 1919 and was published quarterly by the National Child Labor Committee until 1984. The objective of the publication was to address issues related to child labor and advocate for the rights of children in the workforce. It included articles, research and opinion pieces that provided a comprehensive analysis of child labor practices, legislation, and social implications. It also highlighted the efforts made by organizations, activists, and policymakers to eradicate child labor and promote the welfare and education of children.
