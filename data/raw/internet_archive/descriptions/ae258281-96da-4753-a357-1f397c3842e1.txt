The Anti-Slavery Bugle was a historical publication that covered abolitionism and the anti-slavery movements in the United States. It was established in 1845 and was published weekly by the Ohio American Antislavery Society. The Bugle was an abolitionist periodical dedicated to promoting the anti-slavery cause and advocating for the emancipation of enslaved peoples. It included articles on the moral, political, and religious aspects of slavery, reports on anti-slavery meetings and events, speeches and sermons by notable abolitionists, and thought-provoking essays on justice and human rights.
