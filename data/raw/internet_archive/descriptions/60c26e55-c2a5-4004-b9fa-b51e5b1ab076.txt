Foreign Policy Reports was a publication covering foreign policy updates. It was established in 1925 and was published bimonthly by the Foreign Policy Association. The Reports focused on international relations, economic policies, and diplomatic issues primarily in the context of post-World War I Europe. Its scope included analysis of reparations, war debts, and economic stabilization efforts, offering articles that examined the financial, political, and social implications of these topics. The publication featured reports, scholarly articles, and policy analyses by experts, governments, and financial institutions, aimed at providing a comprehensive understanding of the complex process of Europe's economic reconstruction and debt management. Regular subjects covered included the mechanics of reparations, the financial policies of European nations, international negotiations on war debts, and proposals for the commercialization and settlement of war obligations.
