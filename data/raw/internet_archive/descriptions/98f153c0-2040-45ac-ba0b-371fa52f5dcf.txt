The World At War (1973) Producer - Thames Television - Jeremy Isaacs Director - David Elstein Narrator - Laurence Olivier Composer - Carl Davis 1 Series - 26 Episodes Run Time - 22 hours 32 minutes Original broadcast network - ITV Original Release 31 October 1973 - 8 May 1974 The World at War is a 26-episode British documentary television series that chronicles the events of the Second World War. It was produced in 1973, at a cost of £900,000 (equivalent to £11,600,000 in 2021), the most expensive factual series ever produced. It was produced by Jeremy Isaacs , narrated by Laurence Olivier and included music composed by Carl Davis . The book, The World at War, published the same year, was written by Mark Arnold-Forster to accompany the TV series. The World at War attracted widespread acclaim and now it is regarded as a landmark in British television history.
