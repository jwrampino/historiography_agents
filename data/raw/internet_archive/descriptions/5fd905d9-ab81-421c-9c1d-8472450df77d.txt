The BMJ is an active peer-reviewed medical journal published weekly by The BMJ Publishing Group (BMJPG), the publishing division of the British Medical Association (BMA). Launched in 1840 as the Provincial Medical and Surgical Journal , it merged with the London Journal of Medicine in 1852 to form the Associated Medical Journal. Renamed the British Medical Journal in 1857, with volume numbering restarting at 1, it was shortened to BMJ in 1988 before acquiring its current name in 2014, and is one of the oldest extant general medical journals in the world. Each issue features original articles covering topics in clinical medicine, public health, health care policy, medical education, or biomedical research. It also contains clinical reviews, education resources, information and discussions on recent medical advances, rapid responses, and editorial perspectives, as well as analysis papers that address topical clinical, scientific, ethical, and policy issues of interest to doctors, patients and health policy makers. Since 1982, a special two-week “Christmas Edition” issue has been produced annually, containing more light-hearted research papers, along with features, essays, comment, and other article types.
