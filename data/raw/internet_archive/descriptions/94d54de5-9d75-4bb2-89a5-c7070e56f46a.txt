The Labor Information Bulletin was a publication covering labor and employment information in the United States. It was established in 1934 and was published monthly by the U.S. Department of Labor, under the purview of the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS). The Bulletin provided timely and accessible summaries of data and legislative information important to wage earners, employers, and government officials. It translated complex statistical studies and legal decisions into easily digestible articles. The publication featured analysis and updates on significant labor laws, such as the Fair Labor Standards Act of 1938 (FLSA), which established minimum wage, overtime pay, recordkeeping, and child labor standards as well as information and statistics regarding employment trends, wage rates, hours of labor, and weekly earnings across various industries. It also included reports on the activities of agencies like the National Labor Board, covering topics such as industrial elections, dispute settlements, and the use of injunctions in labor disputes and articles related to safety standards in manufacturing, industrial injuries, and health protections for workers (e.g., silicosis prevention).
