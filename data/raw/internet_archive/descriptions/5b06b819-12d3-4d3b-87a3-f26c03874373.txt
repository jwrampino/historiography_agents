Released in October 1995, Ken Loach’s Land and Freedom charts the experiences of idealistic, unemployed Liverpudlian David Carne, who joins the fight against Fascism in the early days of the Spanish Civil War. Enmeshed in his own side’s political infighting, he learns the compromise and disillusionment of civil war. Critics and viewers noted the similarity between the story narrated in the film and George Orwell's book Homage to Catalonia , in which the author wrote one of the more famous accounts of the war, that of his own experience as a volunteer in the ILP Contingent, part of the POUM militia. Following interest in Land and Freedom , Abel Paz, a militant in the Spanish Civil War and author of Durruti in the Spanish Revolution , went on a tour of dozens of public meetings in Italy.
