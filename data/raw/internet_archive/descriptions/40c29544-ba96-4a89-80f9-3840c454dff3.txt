Tadhana: A History of the Filipino People is an eight-volume encyclopedic work commissioned by the Marcos Foundation and collaborations with Filipino Historians at that time in order to standardize Philippine History, without foreign influences. Volume One (Parts One and Two) focuses on the prehistory of the Philippines; Volume Two (Parts One and Two) focuses on pre-1521 Philippine History and the entire Spanish Colonial Era (1521 - 1860); Volume Three (Parts One and Two) focuses on the Philippine Revolutionary Era and the American Colonial Period; Volume Four (Parts One and Two) focuses on the Commonwealth and WWII periods, the Old Society Period of the Phillipines (1946 - 1965), and the Period of the New Filipino (1965 - 1972) and the New Society (1972 - 1980). This book [Volume One, Part One] focuses on defining the origins of the Philippines and its geological and territorial characteristics.
