50 YEAR ANNIVERSARY of UNEF 1  The first peacekeeping force In March 1957, the force facilitated the departure of Israeli forces from Gaza and Sharm-el-Sheik. (per Wikipedia :UNEF 1 History) . This was the last phase of the Armistice between Egypt and Israel incl the withdrawal of their troops as well as the troops of England and France. So March 57 is generally recognized as the day UNEF 1 the day the 6000 troops from 10 nations became operational   United Nations Emergency Force  ( UNEF 1 ) was established by the United Nations General Assembly  to secure an end to the Suez Crisis   UNEF 1 owes its inspiration from theory into reality  as a result of efforts by UN Secretary- General Swedish Dag Hammarskjold and Canadian   Minister of External Affairs and Nobel Peace Prize 1 957 Lester B Pearson . Their  proposal and effort from Canadian   Minister of External Affairs Lester B Pearson  . The General Assembly  approved a plan   submitted by the Secretary-General  in 1956 which envisaged the deployment of UNEF on both sides of an armistice line. (  with resolution 1001 (ES-I) on November 7, 1956.) After multilateral negotiations with Egypt, eleven countries offered to contribute to a force on the Egyptian side of the armistice line.   The 10 countries that offered troops were Canada ,Brazil,.Indonesia,Columbia, Sweden,Denmark,India,Finland,Yugoslavia ,Norway. Seven countries stayed the 10 years - 3 dropped out for various reasons during the mandate (1957 1967) First Columbia, then ,Indonesia then Finland. Poland is mentioned as 11th member  in Wikipedia as a member but to the best of my knowledge did not contribute "boots on the ground" on UNEF 1 10 year tenure.   UNEF was the first UN military force of its kind- now 50 years later - was it worth it?
