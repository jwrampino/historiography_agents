The Arena, founded by Benjamin Orange Flower in 1889 was a monthly literary and political review dedicated to discussing progressive social and political issues. It was established in 1889 and was published in the United States by Arena Publishing Co until 1909. The magazine served as a platform for a wide range of progressive and reformist ideas, openly supporting birth control, free silver, agrarian reform, the single tax, and efforts to break up large trusts. Articles promoted social reform, publishing articles on poverty, slum conditions, sweatshops, child labor, and other contemporary social issues. It later became known for its investigative journalism as well as its writing on socialism and support for trade union organizing.
